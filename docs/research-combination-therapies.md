# Combination Therapies for Enhanced Hair Regrowth

**Research compiled:** January 2026

This document summarizes peer-reviewed evidence on combining photobiomodulation (PBM/LLLT) with other therapies to enhance follicle regrowth outcomes.

---

## Executive Summary

The research strongly supports **multimodal therapy** for best results:

| Combination | Efficacy Ranking (SUCRA) | Evidence Level |
|-------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| Minoxidil 5% + Microneedling | 95.8% | Strong (meta-analysis) |
| Minoxidil 5% + PRP | 64.7% | Moderate |
| Minoxidil 5% alone | 53.9% | Strong |
| PRP alone | 34.9% | Moderate |
| Microneedling alone | 27.8% | Moderate |

**Key finding:** Minoxidil + Microneedling showed +13 hairs/cm² improvement over minoxidil alone.

## 1. Near-Infrared (NIR) Light: 850nm

### Scientific Rationale

NIR light (760-1000nm) shows equal or greater efficacy than red light for PBM. The absorption spectrum of cytochrome c oxidase (CCO) in mitochondria has two peaks:
- **Red peak:** ~660nm
- **NIR peak:** ~830-850nm

Both wavelengths stimulate the same pathway (ATP production, ROS modulation) but NIR penetrates deeper.

### Depth of Penetration

| Wavelength | Penetration Depth | Target |
|------------|-------------------|--------|
| 650nm (Red) | 1-2mm | Superficial follicles, early thinning |
| 850nm (NIR) | 3-4mm | Deep follicles, advanced miniaturization |

### Clinical Evidence

- One study using 830nm showed greater efficacy than 632, 670, and 785nm wavelengths
- Commercial devices increasingly use dual-wavelength (650nm + 850nm) approach
- NIR reduces inflammation and enhances mitochondrial function in deeper tissue

### Recommendation for Roxy RedLight

**Add 850nm NIR LEDs** as a secondary array:
- Mix ratio: ~70% red (650nm), ~30% NIR (850nm)
- Or: Separate zones for red/NIR with independent control
- Allows targeting both superficial and deep follicles

## 2. Microneedling

### Mechanism of Action

Microneedling creates controlled micro-injuries that:
1. **Stimulate wound healing response** - releases growth factors
2. **Trigger neovascularization** - improves blood supply to follicles
3. **Induce collagen remodeling** - improves scalp tissue health
4. **Activate Wnt/β-catenin pathway** - same pathway as PBM

### Clinical Evidence

- Network meta-analysis (27 trials, 1,110 patients): Microneedling + Minoxidil ranked #1
- +13 hairs/cm² improvement over minoxidil alone (p < 0.05)
- Initiates new anagen phase in resting follicles

### Protocol (from clinical studies)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Needle length | 1.0-1.5mm |
| Frequency | Weekly (not daily!) |
| Duration | 10-15 min per session |
| Timing with PBM | Microneedle first, then PBM same day or next day |
| Timing with topicals | Apply minoxidil 24h after microneedling |

### Synergy with PBM

- Microneedling creates micro-channels improving light penetration
- Both activate Wnt/β-catenin signaling (additive effect)
- PBM's anti-inflammatory effect may reduce microneedling recovery time
- Combined approach showed better results than either alone in case series

### Recommendation

**Add microneedling to protocol:**
- Use derma roller (1.0mm) or dermapen weekly
- Apply PBM therapy immediately after or next day
- Wait 24 hours before applying any topicals

## 3. Minoxidil

### Mechanism

- Potassium channel opener
- Increases blood flow to follicles
- Prolongs anagen phase
- Takes 4-8 months for full effect

### Synergy with PBM

Clinical trial (Esmat et al., 2017):
- Minoxidil alone: effective at 4 months
- PBM alone: effective at 4 months
- **Combination: effective at 2 months** (earlier response)

"LLLT and minoxidil had similar efficacy in hair growth and combination therapy was even more effective."

### Protocol

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Concentration | 5% (men), 2-5% (women) |
| Application | 1mL twice daily |
| Timing with PBM | Apply after PBM session (clean scalp) |
| Duration | Indefinite (stops working if discontinued) |

### Recommendation

**Combine minoxidil with PBM device:**
- Use PBM in morning (clean scalp)
- Apply minoxidil in evening
- Or: Use minoxidil, wait 4+ hours, then PBM

## 4. Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)

### Mechanism

PRP contains concentrated growth factors:
- **PDGF** (Platelet-Derived Growth Factor)
- **VEGF** (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor)
- **IGF-1** (Insulin-like Growth Factor 1)
- **FGF** (Fibroblast Growth Factor)

These directly stimulate follicle stem cells and improve vascularization.

### Clinical Evidence

- SUCRA ranking: 64.7% when combined with minoxidil
- "PRP may confer an early therapeutic benefit before the effects of minoxidil can be fully realized"
- Triple therapy (Minoxidil + PRP + Microneedling) showed highest satisfaction

### Limitations

- Requires clinical procedure (blood draw, centrifugation, injection)
- Expensive ($500-2000 per session)
- Protocols vary widely (no standardization)
- DIY not feasible

### Recommendation

**Consider PRP as clinical adjunct:**
- 3-4 sessions initially, then maintenance every 6-12 months
- Combine with home-based PBM + minoxidil for best results
- Not a DIY option

## 5. Topical Peptides and Growth Factors

### Key Peptides with Evidence

| Peptide | Mechanism | Clinical Result |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Copper Peptide (GHK-Cu)** | Angiogenesis, DHT reduction | +27% hair density at 6 months |
| **Acetyl Tetrapeptide-3** | Strengthens ECM, hair anchoring | +17% hair diameter, +67% growth activity |
| **Biotinoyl Tripeptide-1** | Enhances keratin production | -58% hair loss, +35% density |

### Commercial Peptide Serums

Many serums combine multiple peptides with:
- Caffeine (improves penetration)
- Biotin (keratin precursor)
- Niacinamide (anti-inflammatory)

### Evidence Level

"While early clinical observations suggest copper peptides can reduce hair shedding and improve hair density, extensive large-scale trials remain somewhat limited."

### Recommendation

**Consider peptide serum as adjunct:**
- Apply on non-PBM days or before bed
- Look for: Copper peptides, Acetyl tetrapeptide-3, Caffeine
- Moderate cost ($30-80/month)

## 6. Topical Caffeine

### Mechanism

- Penetrates via hair follicles within 20 minutes
- Stimulates hair matrix keratinocytes
- May counteract testosterone-induced suppression
- Improves scalp microcirculation

### Clinical Evidence

Open-label RCT comparing caffeine 0.2% vs minoxidil 5%:
- Both showed significant improvement at 6 months (p < 0.01)
- No significant difference between groups
- Caffeine comparable to minoxidil for hair maintenance

### Advantages

- No systemic side effects (unlike minoxidil)
- OTC availability
- Can be combined with other topicals

### Recommendation

**Consider caffeine-based topical:**
- Alternative to minoxidil for those with side effect concerns
- Can combine with peptide serums
- Apply to clean scalp before or after PBM

## 7. Proposed Optimal Protocol

Based on the research, here's a synergistic multi-modal approach:

### Weekly Schedule

| Day | Morning | Evening |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **Monday** | Microneedling (1.0mm) | Rest (no topicals for 24h) |
| **Tuesday** | PBM session (20 min) | Minoxidil 5% |
| **Wednesday** | Caffeine/peptide serum | Minoxidil 5% |
| **Thursday** | PBM session (20 min) | Minoxidil 5% |
| **Friday** | Caffeine/peptide serum | Minoxidil 5% |
| **Saturday** | PBM session (20 min) | Minoxidil 5% |
| **Sunday** | Rest | Minoxidil 5% |

### Clinical Add-ons (Optional)

- **PRP injections:** Every 4-6 weeks for first 3-4 sessions, then every 6-12 months
- **Finasteride (men):** 1mg daily oral for DHT blocking (prescription required)

## 8. Roxy RedLight Design Implications

### Hardware Changes

1. **Add 850nm NIR LEDs:**
   - 20-30 units interspersed with red LEDs
   - Separate GPIO control for independent intensity
   - Approximately 70/30 red/NIR ratio

2. **Dual-channel driver:**
   - PWM control for each wavelength
   - Allows pulsed protocols (some evidence for enhanced efficacy)

### Firmware Changes

```c
#define RED_LED_PIN 3
#define NIR_LED_PIN 5

// Treatment modes
enum Mode {
    RED_ONLY,      // 650nm - maintenance
    NIR_ONLY,      // 850nm - deep treatment
    DUAL_WAVE,     // Both - comprehensive
    PULSED         // Alternating - experimental
```

## 9. Evidence Gaps and Cautions

### What We Don't Know

1. **Optimal wavelength ratio** - No study directly compares red/NIR mixtures
2. **Pulsed vs continuous** - Limited evidence on pulsing protocols
3. **Long-term PBM outcomes** - Most studies ≤26 weeks
4. **Synergy mechanisms** - How exactly do these therapies interact?

### Cautions

- **Microneedling:** Proper sterilization critical; infection risk
- **Minoxidil:** Systemic absorption can cause hypotension, unwanted facial hair
- **Overtreatment:** More is not better; biphasic dose-response means too much light can inhibit
- **Expectations:** Even best combinations show modest effect sizes (~17-18 hairs/cm²)
